My Background / About Me
I’m a latino first-generation roboticist with a Master’s degree in Robotics and a Bachelor’s in Mechanical Engineering. My main field of study while pursuing my Master’s degree was Human-Robot Interaction (HRI), where I focused on creating and implementing ways to assist individuals with impairments. My thesis focused on two main areas: helping children with typical development by implementing a robot to encourage physical movement, and assisting children with cognitive and motor impairments by implementing a teleoperated robotic system to help them perform Activities of Daily Living (ADL) and Instrumental Activities of Daily Living (IADL).

    Work Experience
    Research Assistant Oregon State University, Corvallis OR    09/2022 - 07/2024
    • Research focused on robotics for the visually, motor, and cognitively impaired and encouraging physical activity.
      • Designed 3D housings that enhanced the usability of prior vision-impaired devices by 40% and reduced the physical size by 20%. The wiring assembly, sensors, and electronics were redesigned and soldered to suit the housing dimensions.
      • Developed digital and physical controllers using rapid prototyping techniques, aimed at assisting children with cognitive and motor impairments. The digital controllers, crafted in Python (PyQT) and Linux, were tailored to the unique needs of individuals through collaborative efforts across various fields of study. The system was used in a real-life occupational therapy environment and was evaluated by an experienced Occupational therapist where it increased the participant's mobility by 60%.
    Teacher Assistant     Oregon State University, Corvallis OR  09/2022 - 06/2024
    • Instructed and educated undergraduate students in the function, operation, and application of common mechanical engineering instruments, measurement principles, and statistical analysis.
      • Using the Arduino platform, I taught undergraduates programming in C++. This included enabling a system to communicate with various sensors and components, such as DC motors, servos, LEDs, and LCD screens, and implementing sensors like moisture, sound, light, temperature, and motion to detect different types of data, including analog and digital. I also taught students how to read PWM signals and work with DC and AC systems.

    • Instructed study on how to use the fundamentals of Linux, Git, and Python in a robotics course.
      • For 10 weeks, I taught undergraduates to use Python, Git, and the Linux operating system to interact with robots. This involved implementing sensors, such as motion sensors, time-of-flight sensors, Lidar, and accelerometers, to program mobile and quadruped robots and collect data from real robot systems.

    • Taught and proctored undergraduates on modeling and analysis of linear continuous systems in time and frequency domains where they learned the fundamentals of single-input-single-output control system design.
